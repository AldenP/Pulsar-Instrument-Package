Pulse Timing and Polarization of Pulsars

Summer 2024 - Fall 2024 Senior Design Group 3

Group Members: Vincent Miller (PSE), David Patenaude (PSE & CpE), Ethan Tomczak (EE), David Urrego (CpE)

Sponsor: Dr. Stephen Eikenberry (Stephen.Eikenberry@ucf.edu)

Reviewers: Prof. Mark Maddox, Dr. Sonali Das, Dr. Stepehn Eikenberry


Our project is to make an instrument that can measure the polarization and pulse timing of a pulsar. Pulsars are a spinning neutron star that emit electro-magnetic (EM) radiation, typically in the radio wavelengths. Our goal is to design for radiation in the visible spectrum, which is emitted by the Crab Pulsar. This data can be useful in understanding pulsars more, as they have many fascinating properties.
